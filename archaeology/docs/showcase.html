The Journey

Four modes. One archaeological method.

Each mode builds on the last. Survey to understand, reflect on your process, preserve what matters, then see the whole picture.

S

Survey

"First, you listen."

Survey scans a project's conversation history and scores every knowledge domain against it. Primary keywords are weighted 3x, secondary keywords 1x, with per-session caps to prevent any single conversation from dominating the signal. The result is a ranked map of where your project's real expertise lives.

Each domain gets a signal strength (strong, moderate, weak, none) and a confidence level based on how many distinct sessions contributed evidence. A single-session project can never claim "strong" signal — the scoring contract enforces intellectual honesty.

W

Workstyle

"Then, you reflect."

Workstyle analyses how you work with Claude, not just what you build. It parses every session through a seven-step pipeline (W1–W7): metadata extraction, dimension scoring, representative session sampling, and sub-agent deep dives into instruction style, feedback patterns, session arcs, and complexity handling.

The output profiles your dominant working pattern (plan-first, iterative refinement, deep exploration, delegation-heavy, or direct execution), your tool usage fingerprint, delegation preferences, communication style, and how all of these have evolved over time. It even detects preferences that contradict your CLAUDE.md configuration.

C

Conserve

"Now, you preserve."

Conservation extracts the stories worth keeping. Five parallel agents each search for a different narrative type — shipments (things delivered), decisions (choices made), incidents (things that broke), discoveries (surprises found), tales (stories with arcs), and practices (processes that emerged). Each agent reads selected sessions and returns structured artifacts in XML format.

Every artifact is an atomic narrative object: 150–300 words with YAML frontmatter, a unique URI (arch://project/art-001), confidence scoring grounded in source evidence, and editorial significance weighting for curation. Artifacts are assembled into an exhibition — a curated reading of the project's history, grouped by type.

E

Excavation

"Finally, you see the whole picture."

Excavation is the cross-project portfolio scan. A bash script (compatible with bash 3.2) discovers every project with Claude Code history, then launches concurrent claude -p subprocesses to survey each one. It respects freshness thresholds — projects surveyed within the last 7 days are skipped by default.

The results are synthesised into a portfolio report: a ranked table of all projects, cross-project domain patterns (which domains appear in multiple projects and what that means), and a prioritised list of recommended next steps. This is the view from above the dig site — every project laid out, every signal visible.

The Framework

Engineered for confidence

Every output is schema-driven, validated, and designed for progressive consumption.

Schema-Driven

Four versioned schemas govern all output: Domain File Schema v1.0, Finding Object Schema v2, Workstyle Object Schema v1, and Artifact Object Schema v1. Every field, every type, every enum is specified.

6 artifact types · 9 finding fields · 7 workstyle dimensions

Validated

Automated validation scripts (bash 3.2 compatible) check output structure, frontmatter integrity, cross-references, and content contracts. Conservation alone runs 78 checks per project.

78 checks · bash 3.2 compat · idempotent reruns

Progressive Disclosure

A 6-tier reading path means you never read more than you need. Route at INDEX.md, orient at SUMMARY.md, understand at patterns.md, narrate at exhibition.md, act on findings.json, automate from workstyle.json.

6 reading levels · stop-here-if guidance · AI + human paths

Central Registry

A global artifacts-registry.json tracks every artifact across every project with tag and type indices. Cross-project discovery is a single JSON parse, not a filesystem crawl.

tag_index · type_index · URI-addressable artifacts

Artifact types

Six narrative types, each with natural sections that guide extraction agents.

Shipment

A feature, launch, or milestone delivered. Sections: what was built, why it matters, the result.

Decision

A choice made and why. Sections: the options, the constraints, the choice, what happened next.

Incident

Something broke and what we learned. Sections: what broke, the response, the fix, the systemic change.

Discovery

A surprise, insight, or busted assumption. Sections: the assumption, the evidence, the new understanding.

Tale

A story worth telling for its arc. Sections: the setup, the complication, the resolution.

Practice

How we work — a process or workflow. Sections: what we do, why it emerged, what problem it solves.

Deeper Strata

Modes planned for the layers below.

Deep Dive

Detailed investigation of specific artifacts or findings. Follow a single thread through every session it touched, reconstruct the full timeline, surface the evidence chain.

Curate

Editorial curation of exhibitions. Reorder, annotate, connect, and write connective tissue between artifacts. Turn raw conservation into a crafted narrative.

Cross-Project Pattern Detection

Go beyond portfolio listing. Detect recurring patterns, decisions, and practices that span projects. Surface the meta-patterns: the decisions you make over and over, the practices that spread.
